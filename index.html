If you feel like settling down with a good book, I have three recommendations. The first: Frederick Douglass: Prophet of Freedom, by David Blight. All of the information about Douglass you could ever want is found within.
The Guns of August by Barbara Tuchman is one of the greatest works that I've ever read. It is a thrilling and scholarly chronicle of the events leading to the outbreak of WWI and the the first month of military engagements.
Finally, we have the Personal Memoirs and Selected Letters of Ulysses S. Grant. A magnificent writer, Grant displayed the coolness of pen that he showed on the battlefieds of Mississippi, Tennessee, and, my country of origin, Virginia.
